Why so small? Pre-IRA credit rates ($10–50/ton) rarely penciled for new projects. The original 75 MMt cap was exhausted by 2020. Nearly all volume went to Class II EOR, not dedicated storage.
What changes after 2024? IRA-era rates ($60–85/ton storage, $180/ton DAC) plus ExxonMobil's 14 MTA contracted pipeline and Tallgrass hub commissioning drive the step-change.
Uncertainty is real. 270+ projects announced, 19 operating. EPA proposed eliminating Subpart RR (the MRV backbone) in 2025. Class VI permitting runs 2–4 years per well.

Context
45Q as a percentage of total clean energy credits (solar ITC + wind PTC + battery ITC + 45Q) by year.
Structural gap: Solar ITC = 30% of capital × hundreds of billions installed. 45Q = $85/ton × 12 MMmt. The math is inexorable — you can't close the gap with a higher rate alone.
OBBBA cliff (2028–29): The One Big Beautiful Bill Act (July 2025) compressed solar §48E and wind §45Y eligibility to placed-in-service by 12/31/2027, creating a sharp drop while 45Q was preserved.
Relative convergence: 45Q's share of the combined credit pool is projected to rise from 2.5% (2024) to ~25% (2029) — not because CCS scales dramatically, but because solar/wind fall off a cliff.
